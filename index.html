Visit Salto del Tequendama
(Tequendama Jump)

The Tequendama waterfall is a natural waterfall in Colombia, located in the municipality of Soacha in the department of Cundinamarca.

The geographical environment of this waterfall is located in the mountainous areas of Tequendama and Canoas that separate the savannah of Bogota from the lower valley of the river of the same name through which runs the high Andean forest and its waters fall from approximately 157 meters over a rocky abyss of circular shape. Administratively, it belongs to the villages of Canoas and San Francisco of Corregimiento 2 of the municipality of Soacha. Part of its waters are also fed by the overflow of the Muña River dam.

According to a Muisca myth, it was formed by the divine action of Bochica, a god or civilizing hero, who taught the Muiscas to spin cotton and weave blankets, in addition to instilling moral and social principles, according to the mythical tradition that he created the Tequendama waterfall with an accurate blow of his staff to evacuate the waters that flooded the savannah of Bogota.

Visit the rock art in its mountains

The municipality and its inhabitants had just begun to discover it at the hands of the urbanization that preceded the industrial development of the 1950s. The enormous excavations that were carried out to build large industrial emporiums began to unearth the archaeological and ancestral wealth of Soacha.

The first human remains of the Cundiboyacense plateau were found there, in the rock shelters of Tequendama. Pictograms, zoomorphic and anthropomorphic stones have been found on the enormous rocks that have witnessed the growth of Soacha. Until the 70's there were 35 rocks with rock art, today there are barely five due to mining exploitation.
